What is the Northern Lights?

The Northern Lights referred to as Aurora Borealis is a natural light phenomenon mostly observed in areas in close proximity to the earth’s polar circles which includes the Arctic and the Antarctic. They occur when particles in space charge energy such as protons reach the earth and ionize the magnetic field in the upper atmosphere producing light.

When is the best time to see the Northern Lights?

The Northern spotlight is visible from September to March only in regions experiencing colder temperatures, at nights with clear sky and zero light pollution.
